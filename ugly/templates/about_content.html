About Ugly Reader

When Google Reader shut down on July 1, 2013, it was pretty frustrating for a lot of us. There are already a whole lot of replacement options out there but none of them seemed quite satisfying to me so I decided come up with my own solution [insert required XKCD reference here]. After a few attempts, I settled on using an IFTTT recipe to email myself and setting up a complicated set of filters in my Gmail account. This was pretty good but it still took a pretty long time and a bunch of steps to add a new feed and that just wasn't going to cut it! That's why I made Ugly Reader.

When you sign up to use Ugly Reader, you grant the servers access to your Gmail account (more details here) so that when the feeds you follow are updated, you get the articles directly in your email client. Instead of emailing them to you and filling up your inbox, Ugly Reader filters the messages so that they end up in a folder called [Ugly Reader]/Feed Title (with Feed Title replaced by the actual title of the feed, obviously). That's it. There aren't any social features, there aren't any CSS animations, and there certainly aren't any ads.

Ugly Reader is an open source project being developed on GitHub and licensed under the MIT license. Feel free to use this version or deploy your own copy on your own servers. If you make any improvements, please consider pushing them back into master code base by submitting a pull request so that we can all benefit from your hard work!

Let me know if you run into problems or have any questions.

Frequently Asked Questions

  1. What information does Ugly Reader collect about me?

    Only your email address and it is stored (encrypted) in a secure database. This will never be looked at, sold or given to anyone and it will only ever be used for identification when you log in. Ugly Reader never downloads or looks at any email messages in your Gmail account.

  2. How does the secure Gmail login work? Does Ugly Reader see my password?

    Ugly Reader uses an industry standard technology called OAuth to authenticate users. The email address and password that you enter are never visible or given to Ugly Reader since the login happens entirely on Google's side. After you grant email access privileges to Ugly Reader, Google returns a code that is used to upload messages and sort them out of your inbox. You can decide to revoke access at any time by clicking here. If you decide to do this, Ugly Reader will no longer have access to you Gmail account so it will stop delivering updates until the next time you login.

  3. If I grant Ugly Reader access to my account, what sort of control does Ugly Reader get?

    By granting Ugly Reader access to your email account, you are giving it complete control over all of your emails. This is the same level of access as you have provided to the email client on your computer or your phone. Unfortunately, there is no way to request only partial access to your account. That being said, Ugly Reader only ever writes emails to your mailbox and files them under specific headers. As mentioned above, Ugly Reader never downloads or looks at any of your email. You can revoke access at any time by clicking here.

  4. Is the name a reference to the Simpsons?

    Nope. You're welcome to interpret it that way though.
